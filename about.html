About the Parish

The faith of St. Thomas, in the Valley

St. Gregorios Orthodox Church is a parish of the Malankara Syriac Orthodox Church serving the Rio Grande Valley. We are a small but faithful community gathered around the altar to keep a tradition that reaches back to the first century.

Who we are

The Malankara Syriac Orthodox Church belongs to the family of Oriental Orthodox Churches, in communion with the Syriac Orthodox Patriarchate of Antioch. Our spiritual roots are with the St. Thomas Christians of Kerala, India — communities that, according to ancient tradition, were founded by the Apostle Thomas when he arrived on the Malabar coast in AD 52.

Wherever the faithful of this tradition have settled, they have carried their worship with them. Our parish exists to give that worship a home here in South Texas, and to be a place of prayer open to everyone in the region.

What is the Holy Qurbana?

The Holy Qurbana — from the Syriac word for “offering” or “sacrifice” — is the celebration of the Holy Eucharist, and the center of our life as a church. We serve it according to the West Syriac rite, following the ancient Liturgy of St. James, the brother of the Lord.

The service moves through prayer, incense, Scripture, and the offering of bread and wine, which the Church believes become the Body and Blood of Christ. It is chanted in a blend of Malayalam, Syriac, and English, so that visitors and lifelong faithful alike can follow and pray.

“Holy things for the holy.”

The St. Thomas Cross

The emblem of our parish is the Mar Thoma Sleeva, the St. Thomas Cross. Its budded arms, the lotus or arch at its base, and the dove descending from above carry the whole Gospel in a single image: the Cross rooted in this world, blossoming into life, crowned by the Holy Spirit. It has marked the worship of the St. Thomas Christians for many centuries.

Visiting for the first time

If you have never attended an Orthodox service, you are still very welcome. You don’t need to know when to stand or what to say — simply come and pray with us. We light candles, we venerate the Cross, and we share a meal together afterward. Come as you are.
