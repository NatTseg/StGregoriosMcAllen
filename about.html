About the Parish

The faith of St. Thomas, in the Valley

St. Gregorios Orthodox Church is a parish of the Malankara Orthodox Syrian Church serving the Rio Grande Valley from Edinburg, Texas. We are a small but faithful community gathered around the altar to keep a tradition that reaches back to the first century.

Who we are

The Malankara Orthodox Syrian Church, also known as the Indian Orthodox Church, is an autocephalous Oriental Orthodox church. It is led by the Catholicos of the East, with its spiritual seat at Kottayam in Kerala, India, and it is in communion with the other Oriental Orthodox Churches. Our roots are with the St. Thomas Christians, or Nasrani, of Kerala, communities that by ancient tradition were founded by the Apostle Thomas when he reached the Malabar coast in AD 52.

Wherever the faithful of this tradition have settled, they have carried their worship with them. Our parish exists to give that worship a home here in South Texas, named in honor of a beloved saint of the Malankara Church.

What is the Holy Qurbana?

The Holy Qurbana, from the Syriac word for offering or sacrifice, is the celebration of the Holy Eucharist and the center of our life as a church. We serve it in the Malankara Rite, the Indian form of the West Syriac liturgy, following the ancient Liturgy of St. James, the brother of the Lord.

The service moves through prayer, incense, Scripture, and the offering of bread and wine, which the Church believes become the Body and Blood of Christ. It is chanted in a blend of Malayalam, Syriac, and English, so that lifelong faithful and newcomers alike can follow and pray.

Our patron saint

Saint Geevarghese Mar Gregorios of Parumala, 1848 to 1902

Our parish is named for Parumala Thirumeni, born Geevarghese in 1848 at Mulanthuruthy in Kerala. Ordained a deacon at the age of ten, he was drawn to a hermit’s life of prayer and fasting, became a monk, and in 1876, at only 28 years old, was consecrated a Metropolitan and given charge of the Niranam Diocese.

He gave himself to a threefold work: shepherding his dioceses, forming clergy, and reaching out through schools and missions without regard to caste or creed. In 1895 he made a pilgrimage to the Holy Land and wrote of it in what is counted among the earliest travelogues printed in Malayalam.

He fell asleep in the Lord on November 2, 1902, and his tomb at Parumala soon became a place of pilgrimage. In 1947 he was declared a saint by the Catholicos, the first canonized saint of the Malankara Church. We believe in his intercession.

The St. Thomas Cross

The emblem of our parish is the Mar Thoma Sleeva, the St. Thomas Cross. Its budded arms, the lotus or arch at its base, and the dove descending from above carry the whole Gospel in a single image: the Cross rooted in this world, blossoming into life, crowned by the Holy Spirit. It has marked the worship of the St. Thomas Christians for many centuries.
